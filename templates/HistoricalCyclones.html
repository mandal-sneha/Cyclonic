Super Cyclonic Storm AMPHAN (May 2020) ▼

Cyclone Amphan originated in the Bay of Bengal and rapidly intensified into a super cyclonic storm. Making landfall near the India-Bangladesh border, it brought devastation with winds over 200 km/h. Amphan caused widespread destruction in West Bengal and Odisha, leading to loss of lives, displacement, and severe economic impact. Coastal towns faced flooding, structural damage, and significant power outages.

Severe Cyclonic Storm NISARGA (June 2020) ▼

Originating in the Arabian Sea, Cyclone Nisarga made a rare west coast landfall in Maharashtra, India. With sustained winds nearing 120 km/h, Nisarga led to significant structural damage and power outages in Mumbai and surrounding areas. This cyclone highlighted the vulnerability of the densely populated western coast to tropical storms.
